Lazy Cooking

This was a staple for me in college- easy to dump in the crockpot in the morning and almost ready by the time you get home- and if you use crockpot liners, then clean up is even easier! It was also warm and comforting on rainy pacific northwest nights. It's a flexible recipe and things can easily be omitted or added depending on taste. Want super spicy salsa chicken? Go for it! Want some beans and corn? Throw 'em right in.

Ingridents

  • Jar of Salsa (any type will work, but would stay away from fruit)
  • 3-4 lbs of Chicken thighs
  • One Packet of Taco Seasoning (or equivalent)
  • 2 cups of dry rice
  • 8oz of Cream Cheese

Steps

  1. Rub chicken thighs with taco seasoning and place in crockpot, pour salsa over chicken.
  2. Set crockpot to low and let cook for 8 hours
  3. At 6 hours of cooking, mix in the 2 cups of dry rice
  4. At 7 hours, put in the cream cheese
  5. After 8 hours, turn off crockpot, stir and shred chicken.
  6. Serve by itself, in tacos, with chips- however best you'd like it!
